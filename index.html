1970s Lab Equipment Turned Retro Pi Terminal by Tom Nardi, 2018.

I was faced with the same dilemma a few years back: Got a 6″ Meade Newtonian for 80$ because the controller was missing. However, this was a certain transition model so no of the available, used or new, replacement controllers were compatible. My quick and dirty hack was ripping an old tape drive apart to scavenge its beefy stepper controller IC, soldering to a stepper motor from a printer and using an overly precise crystal controlled frequency generator to control the speed. This hack cost nothing and worked more precisely than the original RA DC motor.
